Motivation & Corpus

A workshop, not a gallery

[Draft corpus statement — adapt freely:]

My archive is the behind-the-scenes record of my craft and prototyping work: raw materials — wire, wood, plastic, paper — mid-transformation. It includes the cutting, drilling, sanding, folding, and assembling that never makes it into a final portfolio shot, alongside the finished objects themselves. Some images show a single pair of hands at a bench; others document collaborative sessions with several people working side by side. I chose this corpus because I know it intimately — I can tell at a glance whether a caption describing "cutting" is actually describing drilling, whether "wood" is really plastic, or whether the model has quietly hallucinated a tool that isn't in frame. That gap between what I know happened and what the model reports is the real subject of this project.

Approach

Two routes, one archive

The system reuses the Level 1 SigLIP joint embedding as a baseline (retrieve_l1) and adds a captioning layer on top of it. A quantized google/gemma-3-4b-it (4-bit, T4 GPU) generates one structured caption per image, cached once to captions.json on Drive. Captions are embedded with all-MiniLM-L6-v2 and indexed with FAISS (retrieve_l2). At answer time, the top-k captions are handed — cleaned of bracket notation — to a text-only google/gemma-3-1b-it, which is instructed to answer only from what it is given.

Caption schema — and why

The schema is closed-vocabulary by design: free-text captioning invites the model to narrate rather than describe, and makes retrieval noisy because near-synonyms (e.g. "trimming" vs "cutting") fragment the embedding space. Constraining each field to a fixed set of options keeps captions comparable across the whole corpus and keeps the vocabulary of questions and captions aligned.

Limitations
  • Single VLM pass per image, greedy decoding, no self-consistency check — captions are a single sample, not a consensus.
  • Closed-vocabulary schema trades recall of unusual content for consistency; anything outside the listed options gets folded into "mixed" or "unclear."
